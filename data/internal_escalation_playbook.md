# Internal Escalation Playbook for Support Emergencies

This internal-support note summarizes when an active customer emergency should be converted into a broader account escalation.

## When to consider escalation
Escalation is worth discussing when one or more of these conditions apply:
- the emergency has continued for more than 3 hours with no clear resolution
- multiple simultaneous customer problems or tickets are involved
- important outside work such as renewals, migrations, or other initiatives is blocked
- additional coordination outside normal support handling is needed

A Support Manager On-call should be consulted as part of the decision.

## First actions
1. Decide which escalation level is appropriate.
2. Contact the account CSM, since that person acts as the escalation DRI.
3. If no CSM exists, contact the assigned AE, CSE, or regional manager.
4. If no response is received, open the escalation through the Manager On-call path.

## Operational handling
- The on-call Support Engineer should assign the emergency ticket to themselves.
- Add an internal note linking the coordination Slack channel.
- Post updates in the internal channel as they happen.
- At shift handoff, update the next on-call Support Engineer and Manager so ownership continues smoothly.

## Internal communication principles
- keep updates structured and timely
- make ownership visible
- capture the current blocker and next step
- maintain a clear path for customer-facing follow-up without exposing internal-only details
